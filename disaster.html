Recent floods across northern Karnataka saw massive destruction affecting the lives of millions of families in 12 districts where the evacuated people returned home to barren lands. When floods wrecked Shilpa’s ( Name changed) small grocery store in Nagaral,( Bagalkot) in Karnataka, she was already battling the loss of a dear one. Parshuram motivated her to set up the store again by providing basic and necessary material under our ‘Vapsi’ initiative. Parshuram’s Vapsi initiative is about reviving the local economy of a flood-hit area by engaging people in local occupations. It is not just about livelihood but also prevents migration. Millions of families are facing similar situations across 8 flood-hit states of India. Your contributions matters: link Partner Organisation – Anthodaya Social Service Society

Parshuram team is on the ground

Severe monsoon rains recently rummaged through Bihar causing massive flooding in over 13 districts and affecting millions of families. Located near Budhi Gandhak river people of Kanjhi Tola village in West Champaran suffered badly as floods damaged their crops and land. Team Parshuram mobilized and motivated people and brought together over 55 residents to build a check dam over the river. In just one day, using the material that Parshuram provided and locally available resources, they managed to build the dam. Parshuram rewarded the efforts of people by reaching comprehensive and carefully made Family Kits. Parshuram team is on the ground, reaching relief material and working with affected families in Bihar and other states. The need is urgent. Do your bit: bit.ly/FloodsInIndia
