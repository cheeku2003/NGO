Education
Community hall for village children to attend tuition classes

Education has the power to transform people’s lives. With this motto etched in their heart and mind, the villagers of Patrapada in Puri, Odisha, took a step towards knowledge when they decided, under Parshuram’s ‘Dignity For Work’ initiative, that they want to build a community hall. And they were determined from the very beginning that the purpose of the hall won’t be entertainment or social gatherings. It will be a roof under which the village children gather to attend tuition classes. With the motivation of our team and utilizing local resources, within 20 days the community hall was built by a group of 34. For their efforts, they were rewarded with Parshuram’s customized kits of essential items. Moreover, the village women painted the hall and decorated its walls with floral designs for both aesthetic pleasure and a cheerful look. That is why, though located a little away from the settlement, still, the children have new-found enthusiasm for academics and look forward to their classes.

We motivated over 200 parents, teachers, and guardians, who worked for over 20 days

Two schools in Golaghat, Assam were completely damaged by a windstorm last year, creating a lot of difficulty for the students and teachers While children of Upper Langtha M.E School sat in another makeshift room, students of Tinikuria Primary school had to study in the premises of a local temple. When our team met and mobilized people here, rebuilding the schools was the first on the list of what they wanted to work on.. We motivated over 200 parents, teachers, and guardians, who worked for over 20 days, constructing classroom walls and boundaries, using only local resources. Soon they gifted their children a beautiful School. All participants were awarded carefully made ‘Parshuram Family Kits’ for their beautiful efforts! To know more about Parshuram’s flagship initiative- ‘Cloth For Work’, log on to www.Parshuram.org/cfw

Even a small disruption in our daily routine brings a lot of stress

>Even a small disruption in our daily routine brings a lot of stress..now imagine having to suddenly abandon your home and go to a relief camp, after a disaster. Children are the worst hit by the trauma of Disasters. While working in Sangli District in Maharashtra after the floods this year, Parshuram team reached out to over 8 schools and conducted various activities with parents and teachers. Children were involved in sports, singing, and dancing to create a joyful day for them! As an award for their participation, every child received carefully designed Parshuram School Kits having basic school material..! More about ‘School To School’ initiative at www.Parshuram.org/s2s
